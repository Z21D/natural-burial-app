What is Natural Burial?

Natural burial, an ancient practice rooted in human history, embraces the interment of the deceased in a natural setting, free from the trappings of conventional burial customs. This approach encompasses two main methods: one involving biodegradable caskets and the other eschewing vessels entirely, allowing for burial directly in the earth or with biodegradable shrouds. By removing barriers between the deceased and nature, such as embalming and outer burial containers, natural burial fosters a more intimate connection with the environment.

Environmental consciousness lies at the heart of natural burial, motivating individuals to opt for eco-friendly end-of-life practices. Through the avoidance of toxic embalming chemicals and the reduction of fossil fuel usage in processing human remains, natural burials significantly diminish their environmental footprint. Moreover, by utilizing biodegradable burial vessels or none at all, these practices consume fewer resources compared to traditional burials. Many practitioners also integrate carbon offsetting initiatives into the process, such as contributing to tree planting or conservation efforts, further aligning the burial process with sustainable principles.
